## Day 90 — The School of Love

> When Love draws you beyond your own effort, you begin to live from a food that thinking cannot earn.

You may feel, at times, a quiet loosening within yourself—a soft departure from all the ways you once tried to secure goodness. There was a season when you served virtue carefully, watching yourself, measuring yourself, guided by reason and restrained by fear. You tried to do well. You tried to be faithful. And yet something in you remained small, confined within the narrow room of your own striving.

Then Love began to speak.

Not through effort, but through a kind of inner hearing. And when your will turned—even simply—to consider Love, you were not rejected. You were gently led away from the anxious service of self-improvement and brought into another kind of schooling. Here there is no task to perform. No virtue to polish. No ladder to climb.

Here, you are fed.

Love draws you upward by a single gaze. Divine beholding has only one intent: union. In that gaze, thought falls quiet. Work falls away. Words lose their urgency. What remains is a simple being-with.

This love has no origin in you. It flows from the Father and the Son and lives in you as breath. It gives you the capacity to love from within Love itself.

And in that nourishment, you may begin to rest without asking for anything more.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Love feeds me quietly*
>
> Out: *I rest in Your gaze*
