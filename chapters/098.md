## Day 98 — Where Love Joins You

> When the soul yields completely, it discovers that it has always been held within Love’s own life.

The soul begins to see that no work, however noble, can equal the simple generosity of God. Bounty itself—pure giving without measure—is greater than all the good a creature might accomplish in centuries. For goodness in its truest form does not arise from effort; it flows from the inexhaustible nature of God.

When the soul yields into that understanding, something subtle happens. It becomes quietly joined to the will of God from within. Not by discipline or careful striving, but by a union that holds steady regardless of what may come. Joy does not inflate it, and fear no longer unsettles it. The soul rests in a strange simplicity.

It realizes that God simply is.

And beside that immense being, the soul no longer needs to assert its own existence. Nothing is withheld anymore. Nothing is defended. The soul rests in a quiet consent that God is enough.

In that place prayer itself changes. It is no longer a reaching outward or a pleading upward. The soul receives what is already given, as one who rests within the generosity of Love. What it once clung to, it now releases. What it once feared to lose, it no longer tries to secure.

And so the soul lives without anxious concern for itself.

It begins to sense something astonishing: that the gaze of God rests upon it with a tenderness beyond measure. Not because it has earned such love, but because Love cannot help but join itself to the one who has yielded everything back.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Your love holds me*
>
> Out: *I rest within Your being*
