## Day 81 — Without Yourself, Within Love

> *There is a way of living that passes through effort and arrives at being carried.*

At first, the soul learns by imitation. You draw into yourself the life of Christ as best you can—his tenderness, his surrender, his mercy. You allow his way to shape your own, not to prove yourself worthy, but because love longs to resemble what it adores. In this way, you begin to loosen the tight knot of self.

Yet even this holy striving is not the end.

There comes a quiet turning where the soul no longer stands over herself, measuring her faithfulness or managing her growth. She is drawn beyond her own willing, beyond her inner commentary, even beyond the satisfaction of doing well. It is not that she ceases to live, but that she no longer lives from herself.

In that gentle undoing, God becomes her sufficiency.

She finds herself resting without the usual movements—without self-defense, without anxious devotion, without needing to secure honor or avoid shame. Something deeper holds her. She lives, but the source of her life is no longer her own effort or awareness. She is carried in a love that simply is.

This love is not an idea but a communion—enduring, joyful, and tenderly binding. It moves within her like a quiet current, joining what seemed divided, pouring delight without strain.

And in that union, she is without herself, yet more truly herself than ever before.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Love, be my life*
>
> Out: *I release myself to You*
