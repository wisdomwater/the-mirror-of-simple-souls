## Day 99 — One Will

> Love gathers two wills so gently that, at last, they are no longer two.

The soul senses a gaze resting upon it—steady, tender, unwavering. Not because it has proven itself worthy, but because Love cannot help but behold what it has drawn into itself. In that gaze the soul begins to understand something astonishing: the love that holds it is not measured against others. Love does not divide itself. It gives wholly.

And so the soul whispers with quiet wonder: He loves none more than me.

Not from pride. Not from comparison. But because Love, when it gives itself, gives entirely. It does not portion its heart. Each soul touched by it receives the whole generosity of its gaze.

Gradually the soul finds its own will softening inside that love. It no longer presses forward with separate desires. What God wills begins to feel like its own quiet consent. The struggle between “his will” and “mine” fades until the difference becomes difficult to find.

Love has done this.

It has taken two currents and drawn them into one gentle flow. What the soul once clung to—its own preference, its own certainty—has been gathered into a deeper belonging. Nothing is forced. Nothing is taken. Love simply unites what it touches.

And so the soul rests in a simple knowing: the eye of God remains upon it, not as judgment, but as affection that cannot turn away.

Two wills have become one will.

And Love remains.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Your gaze rests on me*
>
> Out: *Our wills are one in Love*
