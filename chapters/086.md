## Day 86 — The Will Returned

> *The deepest surrender is not giving up sin, but giving back the will itself.*

There is a sweetness in love that can intoxicate the soul. Drawn upward by delight, she may think she has reached the summit. The light is warm, the union tender, the joy almost overwhelming. Love carries her so swiftly that she does not see there are still heights beyond sweetness.

Then comes a more terrible and beautiful light.

She begins to behold not what she feels, but what God is. He is. And in that seeing, she perceives that she is not—at least not in the way she imagined. All that she called herself seems thin beside the fullness of divine being. She sees that even her free will was pure gift, placed within her by generosity alone.

In that illumination, something shifts.

She recognizes how her will has wandered, how it has rested in places where God is not. And gently, without violence, she allows it to be lifted. The will that once clung now loosens. She returns it to the One from whom it first came. Not in despair, not in shame, but in clarity.

When the will rests again in God, the old interior war quiets.

She discovers a strange knowing born from unknowing—a vastness within that feels like standing at the edge of endless depth. She cannot find the bottom of her own nothingness, and yet she is not afraid.

For in losing ground, she has found her resting place.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Receive my willing*
>
> Out: *Let it rest in You*
