## Day 84 — From Valley to Mountain

> *Every soul begins in trembling, not yet knowing she is being drawn toward a summit she cannot see.*

There are stages in love, though love itself is one.

At first, the soul awakens in grace like someone startled by light. She turns from what harms her and resolves, as best she can, to live within the commandments of God. She sees the call to love with her whole heart and to love her neighbor as herself, and it feels immense—more than a lifetime’s work. The effort humbles her. She walks carefully, aware of consequence, shaped by reverence and a kind of holy fear.

This is not failure. It is a beginning.

But love does not leave her there.

In time she begins to sense that beyond what is required, there is what is invited. Not merely to avoid harm, but to offer herself more freely. She notices the quiet counsel of Christ—the way He lived unguarded, unattached to status, wealth, or praise. And something in her stirs with longing, not from pressure, but from affection.

Fear once guarded her steps. Now desire begins to move her.

The mountain is high, and the valley still visible below. Yet even in trembling, she is already being drawn upward. Each degree has its season. Each season its grace.

Love is patient with its own unfolding.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Draw me higher in love*
>
> Out: *I release my fear*
