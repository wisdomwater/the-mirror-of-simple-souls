## Day 87 — The Deepness of Meekness

> *In the lowest place, the soul discovers a light she could not see from higher ground.*

When the will has been returned and the sweetness has quieted, the soul falls into a depth she did not expect. She sees the measure of her own nothingness—not as accusation, but as vastness. It feels like standing in a darkness without bottom, unable to find ground beneath her.

This is the throne of meekness.

Here pride cannot play. There is no place for self-display in a soul that has seen the endless poverty of her own being apart from God. She does not despair. She simply knows. And in that knowing, she is low—so low that she begins to see clearly.

From this lowness, she beholds the sun of divine goodness.

Mercy and justice, once feared as opposites, appear reconciled. Her story of waste and wandering is no longer a wound that defines her, but a place where goodness has entered. She is all and she is none—none in herself, all in the One who joins her to Himself.

In this falling, she does not rise again as before. She abides.

Freed from striving, freed even from the need to see herself seeing, she lives now from the knowing of divine bounty. And that knowing loosens every chain.

She has fallen into love’s deep ground, where there is nothing to defend and nothing to prove.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Make me low in truth*
>
> Out: *Lift me in Your goodness*
