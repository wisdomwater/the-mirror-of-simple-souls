## Day 93 — Between Your Nothing and His All

> When you see your smallness beside His vastness, you begin to fall into a different kind of knowing.

You look at yourself and see frailty, confusion, missteps that seem woven into your days. You look toward God and see boundless might, wisdom without edge, goodness without shadow. The contrast feels immeasurable. He is all; you are scarcely anything at all.

And yet something quiet happens in that space between.

You begin to sense that you do not truly comprehend either your weakness or His goodness. If you could grasp one fully, you would grasp the other. But you cannot. Your understanding is a thin thread stretched across an endless sky. Still, even the smallest glimpse of your poverty has opened you to a glimpse of His mercy. The little you see of your need has become the doorway to the little you see of His generosity.

So you turn inward in meditation. Not to argue. Not to defend. Simply to consent.

You find yourself whispering strange prayers: If it pleased You that I had never been, I would rest in that. If it pleased You to correct me with fire, I would accept it. If I possessed any goodness at all, I would lay it back into You and become nothing rather than keep what was not originally Yours.

In this, something shifts. You are no longer trying to secure yourself.

You are simply letting yourself belong.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *You are all goodness*
>
> Out: *I return all to You*
