## Day 80 — The Oil of Peace

> *Peace is poured where the will no longer insists on leading.*

There is a kind of goodness that still strives, even when it is beautiful. It desires to love well, to choose rightly, to remain faithful. And though its works may be noble, there is often a quiet war beneath them—anxieties rising and falling like breath not yet surrendered.

But there is another way the soul may be carried.

When love draws you beyond willing and not-willing, beyond managing your own becoming, something softer begins to flow. It is as though you are set where you once were before you ever chose, before you ever reached. You are worked upon without your effort, shaped by a goodness that began you without your help and now gathers you back the same way.

The One who gave you freedom did not do so with reproach. Even your wandering will came from divine generosity. And when that will turned toward loss, Love did not withdraw the gift but entered it, knitting human frailty to divine mercy, so that nothing would be beyond return.

To “resort there where you were” is not to undo your life, but to rest in the simplicity from which you came—bare, beloved, unguarded. The mind may try to grasp this and fail. Something in you that loves control may not see it.

Yet there is a beholding that comes only when that grasping grows quiet.

And in that beholding, Love beholds Love.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Set me where I began*
>
> Out: *I rest beyond my will*
