## Day 88 — The Book Grows Small

> *What once seemed vast becomes simple when the soul falls into love’s nothingness.*

There comes a place where even the highest words grow thin.

In the sixth estate, the soul sees only God—not by effort, not through created signs, but by a light given directly from Him. Nothing creaturely mediates this knowing. All is gathered into the simple fact of divine being. Love has paid every debt. There is no striving left, no argument to settle. What is, is God.

And yet there is still a seventh hidden within Him.

That final fullness is kept in reserve, not withheld in cruelty but preserved for glory. It cannot be spoken now. It will be known when the soul leaves the body behind, when seeing is no longer through veils.

So the writer pauses.

She confesses that the book which once felt immense now seems small. Words that once blazed with revelation feel like stammering. Before the simplicity of divine being, language becomes almost a kind of exaggeration. What can be said of God is always less than what is.

Love compelled her to write. Love pressed upon her spirit and gave her speech. Yet to those who have truly fallen into love’s deep nothingness, even this writing will seem slight.

For in that place, Reason no longer governs. Explanations fall quiet. The soul sits in peace.

And silence says more than speech ever could.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Beyond my words, You are*
>
> Out: *I rest in silent knowing*
