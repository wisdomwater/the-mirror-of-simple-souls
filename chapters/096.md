## Day 96 — When the Will Falls Silent

> When your own will grows quiet, you begin to live from the will that was loving you all along.

Love comes to you again and speaks with disarming generosity: everything that has ever been or will be rests in Him. His goodness lacks nothing. If you wish to take of Him, He does not refuse. If you wish to receive Him entirely, He does not hold back.

Yet when you hear this, something unexpected rises within you.

You see that what you are, apart from Him, is nothing at all. And what would nothing desire? Nothing has no claim, no separate will to press forward. So you answer simply: you want nothing that does not come from the bounty of Love itself.

In that moment, an old life quietly falls away. The will that once reached outward grows still. The works that once animated you lose their urgency. Even the sweetness that once delighted you fades into a deeper quiet. Not because love has vanished, but because something more spacious has appeared.

You see Him high beyond measure, and yourself low beyond measure—and strangely this does not wound you. It frees you. For you no longer need to raise yourself or secure yourself. You are held within what already is.

Sometimes the soul confuses its own tenderness for God’s love. It feels affection and imagines it is loving God, when quietly it is still circling itself. This too softens with time.

In the unknowing, another life begins.

A life where the soul no longer insists on its own will, and so becomes able to receive the quiet seed of God’s will within it.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Your will lives in me*
>
> Out: *My will falls silent*
