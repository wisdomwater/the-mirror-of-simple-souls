## Day 85 — The Sweetness and the Surrender

> *Love first delights the will, then quietly asks for the will itself.*

In one season, the soul burns with affection for holy works. She longs to offer something worthy to her Beloved, and so she gives what she treasures most—her discipline, her devotion, her labor. She fasts, she prays, she serves, she sacrifices. These works are no burden; they are sweetness to her. To refrain from them would feel like loss. Her will is alive in goodness, and it glows with purpose.

Yet love is subtle.

After she has tasted the beauty of giving, she is invited into something more hidden. Not only to surrender lesser desires, but to relinquish even the joy she finds in her virtue. She lays down not sin now, but preference. She allows her own spiritual hunger to be interrupted. She consents to follow another’s will. This is a deeper martyrdom—the quiet undoing of the self that delights in being faithful.

It feels harder than restraint of the body. It is the surrender of the interior claim.

Then comes another turning.

Love draws her inward into contemplation, where sweetness overflows without effort. Outward labors fall away, and she rests in a tender delight that feels like possession of heaven itself. She melts in union. She believes nothing greater could be given.

For now, this sweetness is enough.

And still, Love is not finished.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Take even my holy will*
>
> Out: *Lead me deeper in Love*
