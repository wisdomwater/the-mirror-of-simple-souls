## Day 95 — The Martyrdom of Will

> Love sometimes asks you to release even the comfort of being loved.

You are pressed with a question that feels unbearable: could you accept it if He loved another more than you? Could you love another more than Him? Could you bear a love that did not circle back to secure you?

Your heart hesitates. Not from rebellion, but from a subtle clinging. You still love yourself alongside Him. And that small thread of self-preservation makes the answer slow. You feel the distress of it. Those who have not stood here cannot know how sharp this edge is.

Yet Love does not withdraw the question. You are brought to the place where your will must loosen its last claim.

At last you whisper: if it truly pleased You—if it rose from Your unshadowed goodness—then I would consent. My will would rest there. I would not seek another outcome.

And in that consent, something in you dies—not your love, but its demand. Not your desire, but its need to be central.

This is the martyrdom of will. The quiet yielding of the self that wanted to be secured by love. The child-heart that longed to be preferred above all.

Righteousness asks what relief you would claim. Mercy asks what comfort you would choose. You find that you want neither protection nor advantage.

You want only what Love wills.

And when that answer forms, Love comes again—full, burning, tender—no longer resisted.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Your will, not mine*
>
> Out: *I yield without fear*
