## Day 94 — Love for Love Alone

> When love becomes pure, it desires nothing—not even heaven—unless it comes from the Beloved’s own will.

You begin to sense a fierce tenderness rising in you. If there were goodness you could possess on your own, you would rather see it vanish than keep it apart from Him. If there were glory offered that did not flow from His heart, you would let it pass. Even sorrow, if it came from His hand, would feel more precious than joy that did not.

You find yourself saying strange things in prayer. That you would rather suffer than wound His delight. That you would rather all creation fall back into nothing than for your will to turn against His. Not from fear. Not from threat. But from love that does not want to fracture the bond.

And even if heaven itself urged you toward reward, if saints and angels pleaded, if glory were placed before you—still you would hesitate unless it came from His own solitary desire. For you want nothing that is not born from the quiet, unforced affection of the Beloved.

This is not bargaining. It is not drama. It is the slow refining of desire until only one thing remains: to belong wholly to the will that loves you.

Then the questions grow too deep for thought. Could He love another more? Could you love another more? The mind falters. Words fall away. You pause, as if standing at the edge of something vast.

And in that pause, love waits without needing to win.
