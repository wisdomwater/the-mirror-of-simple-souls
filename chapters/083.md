## Day 83 — Where Poverty Meets Fullness

> *Your deepest lack becomes the doorway through which divine fullness enters.*

There is a boldness in this soul’s confession: she calls herself all need, all poverty, all lack. Not to condemn herself, but to name the truth of her emptiness. And against that vast emptiness stands the vastness of God—who is all goodness, all generosity, all overflowing life.

If she is truly poor, then only fullness can suffice her.

A cup half-filled cannot quiet an ocean of thirst. So she dares to believe that divine goodness must come to her without measure—not as reward, not as payment for virtue, but because Love cannot bear to leave need unmet. The greater the poverty, the wider the space for mercy.

She begins to see that even her brokenness has become a strange instrument of revelation. Through her weakness, goodness is made visible. Through her hunger, abundance is known. Not because evil is good, but because mercy shines brightest where it is most needed.

Her salvation is not something she manufactures. It is the knowing of divine bounty. And if that bounty is known through her story—through her wounds, her failings, her long ache for wholeness—then even her darkness has been gathered into praise.

She cannot lose her need; it remains the open place. And so she cannot lose mercy either.

Where frailty rests inside Love, fullness quietly pours.

**Breath Prayer**

> In: *Meet me in my need*
>
> Out: *Fill what I cannot fill*
