## Epilogue

Marguerite Porete’s message is both daring and deeply simple: the soul is made for union with divine love, and that union is not achieved through endless striving but through surrender. Beneath all the images, dialogues, and mystical language of *The Mirror of Simple Souls* lies a quiet truth—Love itself is the beginning, the path, and the end of the soul’s journey.

Marguerite believed that the soul gradually passes through many stages of spiritual effort. At first we try to become better. We practice virtues, discipline our habits, and seek to shape our lives toward goodness. These things have their place, and they often mark the beginning of the journey. But for Marguerite they are not the final home of the soul. In time, the soul discovers that even its best efforts cannot create the union it longs for. That union arrives only when the soul releases its anxious grip and allows divine love to carry it.

This does not mean the soul becomes passive or indifferent. Rather, it becomes free. When love governs the heart, goodness flows naturally without the pressure of self-concern. The soul no longer works to secure its worth. It lives because it is already held.

Marguerite described this state as a kind of “nothingness,” though the word can sound harsher than what she meant. It is not the disappearance of the soul but the gentle falling away of the self that must always prove itself. When that self relaxes, what remains is a life quietly joined with the will of God. Two wills become one—not by force, but by love.

This union is not reserved for some distant heaven. It begins wherever the soul consents to rest in love. It begins in small moments of trust. In quiet pauses of surrender. In the willingness to believe that divine love is already closer than our own striving.

So if there is a final invitation in these pages, it is simply this:

Let go.

Let the soul loosen its grip on the endless work of becoming. Let love do the deeper work that effort cannot accomplish. Trust that the gaze of God has never left you, and never will.

And may the peace of that love accompany you as you step forward into the mystery of life—
into the eternity that is already unfolding within this very breath.
